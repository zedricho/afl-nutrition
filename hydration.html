Hydration & Electrolytes

Maintaining fluid balance for optimal performance

Why Hydration Matters

Dehydration can significantly impair both physical and mental performance. In the high-intensity, stop-start nature of AFL, staying hydrated is essential for maintaining speed, endurance, decision-making, and skill execution.

📍 Effects of Dehydration

  • 2% body weight loss: Reduced endurance and increased fatigue
  • 3%+ body weight loss: Impaired strength, speed, and cognitive function
  • Increased risk of heat illness in hot conditions
  • Slower recovery post-exercise

Fluid Intake Guidelines

Daily Hydration

Athletes should aim to stay well-hydrated throughout the day, not just during training.

  • Baseline: ~35-40ml per kg body weight per day (e.g., 3-3.5L for an 85kg player)
  • Monitor urine colour: Pale yellow indicates good hydration; dark yellow suggests dehydration
  • Drink regularly: Don't wait until you're thirsty

Pre-Exercise

  • 5-7ml/kg body weight 2-4 hours before exercise
  • For an 85kg player: ~400-600ml
  • Include sodium if you tend to sweat heavily

During Exercise

  • Aim to limit body weight loss to less than 2%
  • Drink at natural breaks (quarter time, half time)
  • Typical intake: 400-800ml per hour depending on conditions

Post-Exercise

  • Replace 150% of fluid lost (1.5L for every 1kg lost)
  • Include sodium to help retain fluid
  • Spread intake over 2-4 hours for better absorption

Understanding Sweat Losses

Sweat rates vary significantly between individuals and depend on factors like fitness, body size, genetics, and environmental conditions.

Typical AFL Sweat Rates

1-2.5 litres per hour during games, depending on conditions and playing time.

Hot Conditions

Sweat rates increase significantly. Extra attention to pre-hydration and during-game fluid intake is critical.

Calculate Your Sweat Rate

  1. Weigh yourself (minimal clothing) before training
  2. Track all fluid consumed during training
  3. Weigh yourself again after training
  4. Sweat loss = Pre-weight - Post-weight + Fluid consumed

💡 Example Calculation

Pre-weight: 85kg | Post-weight: 83.5kg | Fluid consumed: 500ml
Sweat loss = 85 - 83.5 + 0.5 = 2.0 litres in 90 minutes = ~1.3L/hour

Sports Drinks & Electrolytes

Sports drinks provide fluid, carbohydrates, and electrolytes in one convenient package. They can be useful during and after prolonged or intense exercise.

When to Use Sports Drinks

  • Exercise lasting longer than 60-90 minutes
  • High-intensity sessions or games
  • Hot and humid conditions
  • Heavy sweaters or "salty sweaters" (white residue on skin/clothing)

When Water is Sufficient

  • Low-intensity or short-duration sessions (<60 mins)
  • Cool conditions with low sweat rates
  • When trying to manage energy intake
Product Type Carbs Sodium Best For
Sports drink (Gatorade, Powerade) ~6% Moderate During exercise - fluid + fuel + electrolytes
Electrolyte tablets/powders Low/None High Heavy sweaters, hot conditions, without extra carbs
Coconut water Low Low Natural alternative, but lower sodium than sports drinks
Oral rehydration solutions Low High Rapid rehydration post-exercise or illness

Sodium Matters

Sodium is the primary electrolyte lost in sweat. If you're a heavy sweater or notice salt stains on your gear, you may need additional sodium through sports drinks or electrolyte supplements.

What Players Say

"I used to just drink water but found I was cramping in the last quarter. Adding electrolytes during the game has made a noticeable difference." - AFL Player Survey Respondent
