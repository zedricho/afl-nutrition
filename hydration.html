Hydration & Electrolytes

Maintaining fluid balance for optimal performance

Why Hydration Matters

Dehydration can significantly impair both physical and mental performance. In the high-intensity, stop-start nature of AFL, staying hydrated is essential for maintaining speed, endurance, decision-making, and skill execution (Sawka et al., 2007).

Effects of Dehydration

  • 2% body weight loss: Reduced endurance and increased fatigue
  • 3%+ body weight loss: Impaired strength, speed, and cognitive function
  • Increased risk of heat illness in hot conditions
  • Slower recovery post-exercise

Fluid Intake Guidelines

All recommendations below from Sports Dietitians Australia (2025).

Before Training

About 2 hours before training (or the evening before), aim for approximately 10 mL per kg of body mass, in addition to usual daily fluid intake.

  • For an 85kg player: ~850 mL
  • Include sodium if you tend to sweat heavily

During Training

Aim to drink regularly throughout the session – around 150–250 mL every 15–30 minutes, depending on tolerance and opportunity.

Fluid choice depends on duration and conditions:

  • >60 minutes or hot/humid conditions: Sports drink
  • ≤60 minutes or cooler conditions: Water is generally sufficient

After Training

Rehydrate by replacing fluids and electrolytes lost through sweat. Suitable options include:

  • Water
  • Sports drinks
  • Electrolyte powders
  • Low-fat milk (especially after heavy sweat losses)

Rehydration does not need to occur immediately and can be spread over several hours to restore fluid balance.

Understanding Sweat Losses

Sweat rates vary significantly between individuals and depend on factors like fitness, body size, genetics, and environmental conditions. Typical AFL sweat rates are 1–2.5 litres per hour during games (Shirreffs & Sawka, 2011).

Calculate Your Sweat Rate

  1. Weigh yourself (minimal clothing) before training
  2. Track all fluid consumed during training
  3. Weigh yourself again after training
  4. Sweat loss = Pre-weight − Post-weight + Fluid consumed

Example: Pre-weight 85kg, Post-weight 83.5kg, Fluid consumed 500mL → Sweat loss = 2.0L in 90 mins (~1.3L/hour)

Electrolytes

Sodium is the primary electrolyte lost in sweat. If you're a heavy sweater or notice salt stains on your gear, you may need additional sodium through sports drinks or electrolyte supplements.

Product Type Best For
Sports drink (Gatorade, Powerade) During exercise – fluid + fuel + electrolytes
Electrolyte tablets/powders Heavy sweaters, hot conditions, without extra carbs
Low-fat milk Post-exercise rehydration with protein
