Training Day Nutrition

Daily fueling strategies to support your training demands and recovery

Daily Energy Needs

AFL players have high energy demands due to the combination of skill training, conditioning work, and match preparation. Most AFL players require 3,000-5,000+ kcal on heavy training days, with carbohydrates as the primary fuel source (Burke et al., 2021).

Energy intake should match training demands – higher on heavy training days, lower on rest days. Underfueling can impair performance, recovery, and immune function (Thomas et al., 2016).

"Less of a carb load [on training days], structuring carbs around training. Game day purely about performance." - AFL Half Back Flank

Real Player Example: Christian Petracca

Here's what a typical training day looks like for Melbourne midfielder Christian Petracca (Madden, 2022):

  • Pre-Morning Training: Yoghurt pouch with muesli and coffee
  • Lunch: Sandwich or salad with protein and carbs – often grilled chicken, rice, and salad
  • Post-Training: Recovery shake with protein powder, bananas, peanut butter, and milk – or a high protein muffin
  • Snacks: Pretzels and popcorn throughout the day
  • Dinner: Salmon and vegetables

Role of Fats in Day-to-Day Nutrition

There are no specific fat intake targets designed exclusively for athletes. Instead, general population guidelines are typically used, with fat contributing around 20–35% of total energy intake (NSW Institute of Sport, 2020). Intake should be adjusted based on individual training demands, overall energy needs, and body composition goals.

Important

Intakes below 20% of total energy are not recommended, as they may compromise the intake of essential fatty acids and fat-soluble vitamins (Lohman, Carr, & Condo, 2019).
