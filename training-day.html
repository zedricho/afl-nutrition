Training Day Nutrition

Daily fueling strategies to support your training demands and recovery

Daily Energy Needs

AFL players have high energy demands due to the combination of skill training, conditioning work, and match preparation. Energy needs vary based on training load, body size, and position.

📍 Key Points

  • Energy intake should match training demands - higher on heavy training days, lower on rest days
  • Underfueling can impair performance, recovery, and immune function
  • Most AFL players require 3,000-5,000+ kcal on heavy training days
  • Carbohydrates are the primary fuel source for high-intensity AFL training
"Less of a carb load [on training days], structuring carbs around training. Game day purely about performance." - AFL Half Back Flank, describing how training day nutrition differs from game day

💡 Common Question from Players

One surveyed player mentioned confusion around "caloric intake based on physical output." The key principle: eat more on heavy training days, less on lighter days. Your club dietitian can help calculate specific targets based on your body composition goals.

Fueling for Training

Proper nutrition before, during, and after training sessions ensures you can train at your best and recover effectively.

Pre-Training Nutrition

Eating appropriately before training provides energy for the session and prevents hunger and fatigue.

  • 2-4 hours before: Complete meal with carbohydrates, protein, and low fat
  • 1-2 hours before: Lighter snack focused on easily digestible carbohydrates
  • Less than 1 hour: Small snack if needed - banana, sports drink, or energy bar

During Training

For sessions longer than 60-90 minutes, consuming carbohydrates can help maintain performance.

Post-Training

Recovery nutrition is crucial - see the Recovery page for detailed guidance.

Training Day Meal Examples

Here are practical meal ideas suited to the busy schedules of AFL players.

Meal Option 1 Option 2 Option 3
Breakfast Oats with banana, honey, and Greek yoghurt Eggs on toast with avocado and tomato Smoothie with oats, berries, protein powder, and milk
Lunch Chicken wrap with salad and hummus Rice bowl with beef, vegetables, and teriyaki sauce Pasta with lean mince and tomato-based sauce
Dinner Grilled salmon with rice and steamed vegetables Chicken stir-fry with noodles Lean steak with potato and salad
Snacks Greek yoghurt with fruit Trail mix with nuts and dried fruit Rice cakes with peanut butter and banana

💡 Quick Tip

Meal prep on days off can save time during busy training weeks. Cook large batches of rice, pasta, and protein that can be easily reheated.

What Players Say

"Organisation and setting a routine so I know my stomach won't be upset leading into games." - AFL Half Back Flank, on what has made the biggest difference to his performance

Off-Season vs In-Season

Players adjust their nutrition based on training phase:

In-Season Approach

"Carb load rather than carb conscious decisions" - more structured eating around games and high-intensity training.

Off-Season Approach

"Depending on body composition goals - having carbs around training and continuing high protein intake." More flexibility, but protein remains a priority.
