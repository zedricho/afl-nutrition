Game Day Nutrition

Fueling strategies for before, during, and after matches

Pre-Game Meals

The night before and morning of a game are crucial for ensuring glycogen stores are topped up and you're ready to perform.

📍 Pre-Game Nutrition Goals

  • Maximise muscle glycogen stores (your body's carbohydrate fuel)
  • Ensure adequate hydration
  • Avoid gastrointestinal discomfort during the match
  • Feel confident and ready to perform

The Night Before

Focus on a carbohydrate-rich meal that is familiar and easy to digest.

  • Pasta with lean protein and tomato-based sauce
  • Rice dishes with chicken or fish
  • Avoid high-fat, high-fibre, or unfamiliar foods

Game Day Morning

Eat a carbohydrate-focused breakfast 3-4 hours before the game.

3-4 Hours Before

Larger meal: Toast with eggs, cereal with milk and fruit, or pancakes with honey and banana.

1-2 Hours Before

Light snack: Banana, sports bar, white bread with jam, or liquid meal replacement.

💡 Pro Tip

Practice your pre-game nutrition routine during training sessions first. Game day is not the time to try new foods.

During-Game Fueling

AFL matches are high-intensity and last approximately 2 hours. Fueling during quarter-time and half-time breaks is important for maintaining performance in the final quarters.

Quarter-Time & Half-Time Options

Option Benefits When to Use
Sports drink (Gatorade, Powerade) Carbs + fluids + electrolytes in one Throughout the game
Energy gels Quick, concentrated carbohydrates Half-time or when energy dips
Banana or orange slices Natural carbs, easy to digest Quarter-time breaks
Lollies (e.g., snakes, jelly beans) Rapid energy, familiar Quick boost at breaks
Sports bars (small pieces) Sustained energy Half-time if tolerated
"At quarter time I usually have some sports drink and maybe a banana. Half time I'll have a gel if I'm feeling flat. The club provides most of it which makes it easy." - AFL Player Survey Respondent

Post-Game Recovery Meals

The period immediately after a game is critical for kickstarting recovery. Aim to eat within 30-60 minutes of the final siren.

📍 Post-Game Nutrition Goals

  • Refuel: Replenish muscle glycogen with carbohydrates (1-1.2g/kg body weight)
  • Repair: Support muscle repair with protein (20-40g)
  • Rehydrate: Replace fluid and electrolyte losses (see Hydration page)

Immediate Post-Game (0-60 mins)

  • Recovery shake (protein + carbohydrates)
  • Chocolate milk
  • Sandwich or wrap with protein filling
  • Fruit and yoghurt

Post-Game Meal (1-2 hours)

Follow up with a complete meal containing carbohydrates, protein, and vegetables.

  • Grilled chicken with rice and vegetables
  • Pasta with meat sauce
  • Stir-fry with lean protein and noodles

Watch Out

Post-game celebrations and alcohol can interfere with recovery. If drinking, prioritise eating recovery foods first and rehydrating with water alongside any alcohol.

Away Game Tips

Travel adds extra challenges to game day nutrition. Plan ahead to stay on track.

  • Pack familiar snacks in your kit bag
  • Research food options at the away venue or nearby
  • Bring non-perishable backup options (bars, gels, nuts)
  • Stay hydrated during flights - the cabin environment is dehydrating
  • Request appropriate meal options from the club for team travel
