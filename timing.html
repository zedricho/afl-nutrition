Nutrition Timing

When to eat for optimal performance and recovery

Why Timing Matters

While overall daily nutrition is most important, timing your intake around training and games can help you perform better and recover faster. This is especially relevant for AFL players with multiple training sessions per week and short recovery windows between games.

📍 Key Timing Principles

  • Fuel appropriately before exercise to maximise performance
  • Consume carbohydrates during prolonged/intense sessions
  • Prioritise protein and carbohydrates after exercise for recovery
  • Spread protein intake throughout the day
  • Consider pre-sleep nutrition for overnight recovery

Before Training & Games

What you eat before exercise affects your energy levels, focus, and ability to perform at your best.

Time Before What to Eat Examples
3-4 hours Complete meal with carbs, moderate protein, low fat/fibre Chicken and rice, pasta with lean meat, eggs on toast with fruit
2-3 hours Smaller meal or large snack, carb-focused Sandwich, bowl of cereal with milk, yoghurt with fruit and muesli
1-2 hours Light snack, easily digestible carbs Banana, toast with honey, small muesli bar, sports drink
<1 hour Quick energy if needed, avoid anything heavy Sports drink, few lollies, small piece of fruit

💡 Individual Preferences

Some players perform well training fasted, others need food in their system. Experiment during training to find what works for you - just don't try anything new on game day.

Spacing Protein Intake

How you distribute protein throughout the day can affect muscle protein synthesis and recovery. Rather than eating most protein in one or two meals, spreading it across the day appears more effective.

Optimal Protein Distribution

  • Target: 20-40g of protein per meal/snack
  • Frequency: 4-5 protein serves spread across the day
  • Include post-exercise: Protein within 1-2 hours after training
  • Before bed: Slow-digesting protein (casein/dairy) supports overnight recovery

Less Effective

10g at breakfast, 10g at lunch, 60g at dinner = 80g total but poor distribution

More Effective

25g at breakfast, 25g at lunch, 25g post-training, 30g at dinner, 20g before bed = better muscle protein synthesis

Timing Carbohydrates Around Performance

Carbohydrates are your muscles' primary fuel source. Strategic timing ensures you have energy when you need it most.

High Training Load Days

  • Include carbohydrates at each meal
  • Prioritise carbs in meals before and after training
  • Total intake: 5-8g per kg body weight

Low Training Load / Rest Days

  • Reduce carbohydrate portions
  • Focus on protein, vegetables, and healthy fats
  • Total intake: 3-5g per kg body weight

Game Day

  • Carb-rich meals in the 24-48 hours leading up to the game
  • Pre-game meal 3-4 hours before: focus on carbs, moderate protein
  • Top up with easily digestible carbs 1-2 hours before
  • During game: sports drinks, gels, fruit at breaks
"Generally more consistent carb intake because of bigger and more spread output." - AFL Midfielder, on in-season nutrition approach
"Organisation and setting a routine so I know my stomach won't be upset leading into games." - AFL Half Back Flank, on what has made the biggest difference to his performance

Late-Night Recovery Timing

After night games (finishing 10-11pm), timing becomes critical for balancing recovery nutrition with sleep.

Time After Game Priority Options
Immediate Quick carbs + protein Recovery shake, protein milk, fruit
Within 1-2 hours Light, digestible meal Yoghurt with banana, toast with eggs, smoothie
Before bed Slow-release protein Greek yoghurt, cottage cheese

💡 Key Timing Principle

High GI carbohydrates 2-4 hours before bed may improve sleep onset. Avoid heavy meals, high-fibre foods, and caffeine late at night. For comprehensive sleep and recovery strategies, see the Recovery page.

Sample Training Day Timeline

Here's an example of nutrition timing for a day with an afternoon training session.

Time Activity Nutrition
7:00am Wake up Glass of water
7:30am Breakfast Oats with banana, yoghurt, and honey + eggs (30g protein, 60g carbs)
10:30am Morning snack Greek yoghurt with fruit (20g protein, 25g carbs)
12:30pm Lunch (pre-training) Chicken rice bowl with vegetables (35g protein, 80g carbs)
3:00pm Pre-training snack Banana and sports drink
4:00-6:00pm Training Water/sports drink as needed
6:15pm Post-training Protein shake with banana (25g protein, 30g carbs)
7:30pm Dinner Salmon with rice and vegetables (35g protein, 60g carbs)
9:30pm Pre-sleep snack Greek yoghurt or cottage cheese (20g protein)
